I am currently a Data Scientist working at Ernst & Young, one of the top consulting firms in the world. My main contributions have been in predictive maintenance and anomaly detection projects, mostly designing machine learning algorithms in the context of time series analysis.

I left the Academia in 2019, after I received a PhD in Astrophysics from the University of Melbourne. My Research in theoretical astrophysics was focused on the the study of the dynamics in dense stellar systems known as globular clusters. In particular, I aimed at finding possible dynamical signatures for the presence of intermediate-mass black holes.

In the academic years I worked under the supervision of Prof. Michele Trenti , and collaborated with worldwide experts at the Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics (Cambridge, MA) and the Max Plank Institute of Astronomy (Heidelberg, Ger).
